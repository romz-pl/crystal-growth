# Patents Related to Crystal Growth from the Melt

A systematic survey of patents covering the principal melt-growth techniques: Czochralski (CZ) and variants, Bridgman/Vertical Gradient Freeze (VGF), Float Zone (FZ), Edge-defined Film-fed Growth (EFG), Kyropoulos, Heat Exchanger Method (HEM), Verneuil (flame fusion), Laser-Heated Pedestal Growth (LHPG) / micro-pulling-down (μ-PD), and directional solidification / ingot casting. Foundational (often expired) patents are included alongside representative modern improvements to show the evolution of each technique.

## 8. Laser-Heated Pedestal Growth (LHPG) / Micro-Pulling-Down (μ-PD)

| Patent | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **US 4,040,890** (Haggerty et al.) | Laser-heated pedestal growth apparatus (foundational LHPG patent) | The original LHPG patent: a CO₂ (or similar) laser melts the tip of a feed rod/pedestal to form a small floating molten zone from which a fiber-like single crystal is drawn, without any crucible contact. |
| **US 3,944,460** (Haggerty, Menashi & Wenckus, 1976) | Production of fiber by floating-zone fiber-drawing technique | Early crucible-free floating-zone technique for drawing fine single-crystal fibers, a conceptual precursor/relative to LHPG. |
| **US 4,421,721 / US 5,579,427 / US 5,607,506** | Subsequent LHPG system patents | A series of follow-on patents refining laser-heated pedestal fiber-growth systems (heating optics, feed/pull mechanisms, process control), cited collectively as the lineage of modern LHPG apparatus improvements. |
| **US 10,392,721 B2** | Laser-heated crystal fiber growth system | Enhanced LHPG system for growing single-crystal optical fibers, improving on the original 1970s architecture and its 1980s–1990s successors. |
| **US 11,352,712 B2** (U.S. DOE) | Method for controlling fiber growth in a laser heated pedestal growth system by controlling laser power output, pedestal feedstock rate of motion, and draw rate | Three-parameter closed-loop feedback control (molten-zone height, laser power, draw rate) for producing long, continuous, uniform-diameter single-crystal optical fibers by LHPG; also enables deliberately non-uniform-diameter fibers and continuous operation via feedstock rod swapping. |

*Note: μ-PD (micro-pulling-down) growth — continuous melt transport through a micro-channel in the crucible bottom with solidification just below the crucible — is closely related to LHPG for fiber/rod crystal growth; the two techniques share much of the same patent lineage in oxide fiber-crystal literature (e.g., work by Fukuda, Rudolph, Lebbou & Boulon).*

## Notes on Scope and Sourcing

- This list is a representative, cross-method survey rather than an exhaustive patent-database extraction; each melt-growth technique above has dozens to hundreds of additional incremental patents (dopant control, hot-zone geometry, automation/control-loop refinements, crucible materials, magnetic-field-assisted variants, etc.) held by firms such as GlobalWafers, SUMCO, Siltronic, Shin-Etsu, Saint-Gobain (Saphikon), GT Advanced Technologies, Rubicon Technology, and Crystal Systems, Inc.
- Patent numbers and dates were located via patent-database search (Google Patents, USPTO, Justia Patents) and cross-referenced against the historical/technical literature (e.g., *A Century of Sapphire Crystal Growth*, DTIC; Scheel & Capper, *Crystal Growth Technology*).
- Several "foundational" patents (Verneuil 1910/1911, LaBelle 1969, Haggerty 1976/1977, Schmid 1975) are long expired but are included because they define the core apparatus/method claims from which the entire modern patent families in each technique descend.
