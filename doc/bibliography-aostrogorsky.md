## Notes on Cross-Cutting Works

Several publications integrate both experimental data and numerical/analytical modeling and appear (in abbreviated form) in both parts above:

- The **Submerged Heater Method (SHM)** series (1990–1999) — originating as a numerical proposal (1990) and subsequently validated experimentally on Sn–Bi, Ge, InSb, and GaSb systems — represents Ostrogorsky's central technical contribution to segregation control in melt growth.
- The **SUBSA / Crystal Growth with a Baffle (CGB)** International Space Station investigations (2001–2009) combine ground-based and microgravity experiments with companion numerical models of dopant segregation and residual-acceleration effects.
- The **effective segregation coefficient (k_eff)** theoretical framework, begun with Müller in 1992–1993, was progressively refined through a series of solo-authored analytical papers (2011–2015) revisiting and extending the classical Burton–Prim–Slichter model.
- The **InI (indium iodide) detector-crystal** program (2006–2023), conducted with Croell, Volz, and Riabov, spans crystal growth, thermophysical property measurement, and interface-shape simulation for γ-ray detector applications, continuing to the present day.
