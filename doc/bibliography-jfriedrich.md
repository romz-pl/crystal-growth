# Jochen Friedrich (Fraunhofer IISB) Bibliography

Compiled from Friedrich's own publications and co-authored works (with Georg Müller, Daniel Vizman, Christian Reimann, Matthias Trempa, and others), spanning the Erlangen Crystal Growth Laboratory / Fraunhofer IISB tradition of research into convective phenomena in melt-based crystal growth. Organized chronologically within two categories: **experimental** and **modeling/simulation** work. Some entries combine both aspects, as is characteristic of this group's methodology (numerical results validated against in-situ/experimental data).

---

## A. Experimental Work

1. Rupp, R., Müller, G., Neumann, G. — surface tension / capillary (Marangoni) convection characterization of GaAs melt by the sessile-drop method, in preparation for the D2 Spacelab GaAs floating-zone experiments (Erlangen Crystal Growth Laboratory, late 1980s).

2. Seidl, A., McCord, G., Müller, G., Leister, H.-J. (1994). *Experimental observation and numerical simulation of wave patterns in a Czochralski silicon melt.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 137, 326–334.

3. Cröll, A., Müller-Sebert, W., Nitsche, R., Friedrich, J. et al. (1994). *Floating-zone growth of GaAs under microgravity during the D2-mission.* Crystal Research and Technology, 29(3), 385–393. — Report on the Spacelab D2 mission GaAs floating-zone crystal (20 mm diameter), showing dopant striations from time-dependent Marangoni convection.

4. Friedrich, J., Lee, Y.-S., Fischer, B., Kupfer, C., Vizman, D., Müller, G. (1997). *Experimental and numerical study of Rayleigh–Bénard convection affected by a rotating magnetic field.* (submitted, Physics of Fluids / presented at IUTAM Symposium). Temperature measurements in a cylindrical liquid-gallium test cell under varying Rayleigh and magnetic Taylor numbers, compared against 3D time-dependent simulations.

5. Friedrich, J. et al. — *Study of the impact of magnetic fields to suppress unsteady Marangoni convection* (preparatory investigations for the D2-GaAs Spacelab mission), Erlangen Crystal Growth Laboratory, early–mid 1990s.

6. Vizman, D., Grabner, O., Müller, G. (2001). *Three-dimensional numerical simulation of thermal convection in an industrial Czochralski melt: comparison to experimental results.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 233, 687–698. — Experiment carried out on a Leybold EKZ 1300 CZ puller (WACKER Siltronic), 360 mm crucible, thermocouple measurements compared with STHAMAS3D simulations.

7. Grabner, O., Mühlbauer, A., Muiznieks, A., Virbulis, J., Vizman, D., Friedrich, J., Müller, G. (2001). *Comparison of measurements and numerical simulations of melt convection in Czochralski crystal growth of silicon.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 230(3–4), 481–491. — Direct comparison of measured and predicted thermocouple readings in an industrial silicon CZ melt during real crystal growth.

8. Dadzis, K., Niemietz, K., Pätzold, O., Wunderwald, U., Friedrich, J. (2011). *Non-isothermal model experiments and numerical simulations for directional solidification of multicrystalline silicon in a traveling magnetic field.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 333(1), 7–15.

9. Dadzis, K., Ehrig, J., Niemietz, K., Pätzold, O., Wunderwald, U., Friedrich, J. (2011). *Model experiments and numerical simulations for directional solidification of multicrystalline silicon in a traveling magnetic field.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 333, 7–15. (Companion/extended experimental study.)

10. Kupka, I., Lehmann, T., Trempa, M., Reimann, C., Friedrich, J. et al. (2017). *Influence of different nucleation layers on the initial grain structure of multicrystalline silicon ingots.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 459, 43–48. — Solidification/melt convection effects on nucleation-layer performance.

11. Sylla, L., Friedrich, J., Reimann, C., Meissner, E. et al. (2016). *Interaction of SiC particles with moving solid-liquid interface during directional solidification of silicon.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 446, 51–58. — Terrestrial and microgravity growth experiments studying particle behavior under convective vs. diffusive melt regimes.

12. Friedrich, J., Reimann, C., Jauss, T., Cröll, A., Sorgenfrei, T., Volz, M. et al. (2017). *Engulfment and pushing of Si₃N₄ and SiC particles during directional solidification of silicon under microgravity conditions.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 468, 84–90. — Growth experiments on Earth and under microgravity (isolating convective effects) to determine critical growth velocities for particle engulfment.

13. Sorgenfrei, T., Jauss, T., Cröll, A., Volz, M., Friedrich, J. et al. (2017). *The critical growth rate for particle incorporation during the directional solidification of solar silicon under microgravity.* — Space-based (microgravity) validation of convective-vs-diffusive particle pushing/engulfment models.

14. Trempa, M., Reimann, C., Friedrich, J., Müller, G. et al. — series of G1/G0-scale directional-solidification experiments (2010s) investigating melt-flow-controlled impurity incorporation (O, N, C, metals) in multicrystalline silicon ingots, including:
    - Trempa, M. et al. (2015). *Investigation of iron contamination of seed crystals and its impact on lifetime distribution in Quasimono silicon ingots.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 431, 91–96.
    - Kranert, C., Trempa, M., Reimann, C., Friedrich, J. et al. (2021). *Impact of silicon feedstock contamination by gas phase diffusion on material quality of cast silicon ingots.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 565, 126147.
    - Kranert, C., Ghosh, M., Hamacher, M. et al., Friedrich, J. (2021). *Assessment of residual melt removal as approach to reduce the top redzone of cast silicon ingots.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 572, 126282. — Direct experimental manipulation of residual melt/convective transport at end of solidification.

15. Stockmeier, L., Kranert, C., Raming, G., Miller, A., Friedrich, J. et al. (2018, 2021). *Edge facet dynamics during the growth of heavily doped n-type silicon by the Czochralski-method* and *Facet growth and geometry of the growth ridge during dynamic Czochralski processes*, Journal of Crystal Growth. — Ridge/facet geometry as a fingerprint of melt-flow-driven interface behavior, measured by optical profilometry.

16. Mosel, F., Denisov, A. V., Klipp, B., Friedrich, J. et al. (2019, 2020, 2021 conference series). *Limitations of the growth rate of silicon mono ingots grown by the Czochralski technique* and *Cost effective growth of silicon mono ingots by the application of active crystal cooling in combination with large melt volumes in CZ-puller* and *Influence of an active crystal cooling device on the shape of the phase boundary in mono ingots grown by the Czochralski technique.* — Experimental stability diagrams for buoyancy-driven convection at industrial melt scales.

17. Friedrich, J., Jung, T., Trempa, M., Mühe, A. et al. (2019). *Considerations on the limitations of the growth rate during pulling of silicon crystals by the Czochralski technique for PV applications.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 318(2), 234–238 (numbering per journal issue). — Combines industrial-scale experimental data (up to 300 kg melts, 26″ crucibles) with modeling of convective heat transport limits.

18. Hirsch, A., Trempa, M., Kupka, I., Friedrich, J. et al. (2019). *Investigation of gas bubble growth in fused silica crucibles for silicon Czochralski crystal growth.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 528, 125272. — Convective/thermal gradient effects on crucible bubble evolution.

## B. Modeling and Simulation Work

1. Rupp, R., Müller, G., Neumann, G. (1989). *Three-dimensional time dependent modelling of the Marangoni convection in zone melting configurations for GaAs.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 97, 34–41. — Foundational 3D transient model of capillary convection for the Erlangen floating-zone GaAs program, precursor to Friedrich's own subsequent work.

2. Friedrich, J., Lee, Y.-S., Fischer, B., Kupfer, C., Vizman, D., Müller, G. (1999). *Experimental and Numerical Analysis of the Influence of a Rotating Magnetic Field on Convection in Rayleigh–Bénard Configurations.* In: *MHD Dynamics at the Turn of the Millennium* / IUTAM Symposium series, Fluid Mechanics and Its Applications, vol. 51. Springer.

3. Vizman, D., Grabner, O., Müller, G. (2001). *Three-dimensional numerical simulation of thermal convection in an industrial Czochralski melt: comparison to experimental results.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 233, 687–698. (STHAMAS3D code; also listed under Experimental for its validation component.)

4. Grabner, O., Mühlbauer, A., Muiznieks, A., Virbulis, J., Vizman, D., Friedrich, J., Müller, G. (2001). *Comparison of measurements and numerical simulations of melt convection in Czochralski crystal growth of silicon.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 230, 481–491. — Fine-grid (≈1.9 million control volumes), 3D time-dependent turbulent flow and heat transfer simulation, FFT frequency-spectrum analysis.

5. Neubert, M., Kurz, A., Pusztai, A., Müller, G., Friedrich, J. (1999). *Global temperature field simulation of the vapour pressure controlled Czochralski (VCZ) growth of 3″–4″ gallium arsenide crystals.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 198–199, 349–354.

6. Vizman, D., Friedrich, J., Müller, G. (2001). *3D time-dependent numerical study of the influence of the melt flow on the interface shape in a silicon ingot casting process.* Journal of Crystal Growth. — STHAMAS3D-based Boussinesq simulations for directional solidification/casting geometries, later published in expanded form (Journal of Crystal Growth, 2006/2007).

7. Fainberg, J., Vizman, D., Friedrich, J., Müller, G. — *3D time-dependent numerical study of the influence of the melt flow on the interface shape in a silicon ingot casting process* and *Influence of different types of magnetic fields on the interface shape in a 200 mm Si-EMCZ configuration* (early-to-mid 2000s), demonstrating the effect of electromagnetic Czochralski (EMCZ) fields on interface/convection coupling.

8. Vizman, D., Friedrich, J., Müller, G. — *Numerical study of the influence of different types of magnetic fields on the interface shape in directional solidification of multi-crystalline silicon ingots.* Journal of Crystal Growth. — Steady vertical, horizontal, and combined field+DC current configurations.

9. Fainberg, J., Vizman, D., Friedrich, J., Müller, G. — *Numerical modeling of Czochralski silicon crystal growth* (global model, Large Eddy Simulation, LES) — describes computational model for 3D unsteady turbulent melt convection in CZ systems, verified against melt temperature measurements; addresses oxygen transport and crucible-rotation effects on turbulent structure. *(Global model of Czochralski silicon growth to predict oxygen content and thermal fluctuations at the melt–crystal interface.)*

10. Reimann, C., Trempa, M., Jung, T., Friedrich, J., Müller, G. (2010). *Modeling of incorporation of O, N, C and formation of related precipitates during directional solidification of silicon under consideration of variable processing parameters.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 312, 878–885.

11. Reimann, C., Trempa, M., Friedrich, J., Müller, G. (2010). *About the formation and avoidance of C and N related precipitates during directional solidification of multi-crystalline silicon from contaminated feedstock.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 312, 1510–1516.

12. Trempa, M., Reimann, C., Friedrich, J., Müller, G. (2010). *The influence of growth rate on the formation and avoidance of C and N related precipitates during directional solidification of multi-crystalline silicon.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 312, 1517–1524. — Coupled melt-convection/mass-transport modeling.

13. Kundin, J., Aufgebauer, H., Reimann, C., Friedrich, J., Emmerich, H., Croell, A. (2016). *Phase-field simulations of particle capture during the directional solidification of silicon.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 441, 90–96.

14. Kundin, J., Aufgebauer, H., Reimann, C., Friedrich, J. et al. (2017). *Dynamic Modeling of Critical Velocities for the Pushing/Engulfment Transition in the Si–SiC System Under Gravity Conditions.* Metallurgical and Materials Transactions A, 48, 4732–4740. — Convective flow's influence on the particle pushing/engulfment critical velocity.

15. Derby, J. J., Tao, Y., Reimann, C., Friedrich, J. et al. (2017). *A quantitative model with new scaling for silicon carbide particle engulfment during silicon crystal growth.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 458, 41–47. — Rigorous finite-element modeling correlated against terrestrial and microgravity experiments to isolate convective vs. diffusive transport regimes.

16. Tao, Y., Sorgenfrei, T., Jauss, T., Friedrich, J., Derby, J. J. (2016, corrigendum 2019). *Particle engulfment dynamics under oscillating crystal growth conditions.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 468, 24–27 (and Corrigendum, 522, 2019). — Transient finite-element simulations of oscillating growth rate and its coupling to convective melt behavior.

17. Vizman, D., Fainberg, J., Friedrich, J., Müller, G. — *Numerical study of the influence of melt convection on the crucible dissolution rate in a silicon directional solidification process.* International Journal of Heat and Mass Transfer.

18. Dadzis, K., Vizman, D., Friedrich, J. (2013). *[Traveling-magnetic-field-driven melt convection modeling for directional solidification of silicon].* Journal of Crystal Growth, 367, 77–87.

19. Vizman, D., Dadzis, K., Friedrich, J. (2013). *[Numerical study of TMF-induced melt convection in multicrystalline silicon directional solidification].* Journal of Crystal Growth, 381, 169–178.

20. Friedrich, J. et al. (co-authors from WIAS Berlin) — *Numerical simulation of Czochralski crystal growth under the influence of a traveling magnetic field generated by an internal heater-magnet module (HMM).* Journal of Crystal Growth, 198–199 era work extended in 2007/2008 — global (WIAS-HiTNIHS) plus local transient melt-convection simulations for VCZ and LEC growth of GaAs, validated against measured required heater power.

21. Stelian, C., Vizman, D., Friedrich, J. et al. — *Three-dimensional modeling of melt flow and interface shape in the industrial liquid-encapsulated Czochralski growth of GaAs.*

22. Vizman, D., Fainberg, J., Friedrich, J., Müller, G. — *Large modification of crystal-melt interface shape during Si crystal growth by using electromagnetic Czochralski method (EMCZ).*

23. Friedrich, J., von Ammon, W., Müller, G. (2015). *Czochralski Growth of Silicon Crystals.* In: *Handbook of Crystal Growth* (2nd ed.), Vol. II B, Ch. 45. Elsevier. — Review chapter with a substantial treatment of melt convection (buoyancy, Marangoni, forced, magnetically-damped) in industrial CZ silicon growth.

24. Friedrich, J. (2016). *Methods for Bulk Growth of Inorganic Crystals: Crystal Growth.* Reference Module in Materials Science and Materials Engineering, Elsevier. — Overview chapter covering convective transport phenomena across melt-growth methods (CZ, Bridgman/VGF, floating zone).

25. Friedrich, J., Müller, G. (2019). *Erlangen—An Important Center of Crystal Growth and Epitaxy: Major Scientific Results and Technological Solutions of the Last Four Decades.* Crystal Research and Technology, 55(3), 1900053. — Historical/review article summarizing four decades of convection-related experimental and modeling work from the Erlangen Crystal Growth Laboratory and Fraunhofer IISB, including Marangoni convection suppression by magnetic fields, D2-Spacelab GaAs results, and the development of the STHAMAS3D melt-flow code.

26. Torkashvand, H. [lead], … Friedrich, J. et al. (2025). *[Parameter study of melt flow dynamics / "bulk circulating flow" regime during growth of 300 mm Si crystals by the HMCz technique].* Journal of Crystal Growth, 667 (1 October 2025). — Local, time-dependent 3D LES model of convective heat transport in a 185 kg / 32″ crucible melt, examining magnetic field strength, crystal rotation, and free-surface shear stress as controls on the transition between two-vortex and single "bulk circulating" flow regimes.

27. Friedrich, J. et al. (2024). *Review of simulation and modeling techniques for silicon Czochralski crystal growth.* Journal of Crystal Growth (review article) — Survey covering the evolution of melt-convection modeling approaches (2D global thermal models, 3D LES local melt models, magnetic damping) used by the Erlangen/IISB group and the wider CZ-silicon simulation community.

28. Wang, K., Koch, H., Trempa, M., … Friedrich, J., Derby, J. J. (2021). *Physically-based, lumped-parameter models for the prediction of oxygen concentration during Czochralski growth of silicon crystals.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 573, 126296. — Reduced-order (boundary-layer) convective transport models benchmarked against high-fidelity 2D–3D CZ puller simulations.
