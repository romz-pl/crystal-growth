# Journals for Publishing Crystal Growth Research

A comprehensive list of peer-reviewed journals that regularly publish articles on crystal growth — covering bulk crystal growth, epitaxy, thin films, crystal engineering, nucleation theory, characterization, and related applications.

---

## Core / Dedicated Crystal Growth Journals

### 1. Journal of Crystal Growth (Elsevier)
**ISSN:** 0022-0248 | **Publisher:** Elsevier | **Founded:** 1967

The flagship journal of the field, founded following the 1966 International Conference on Crystal Growth. It offers a common reference and publication source for researchers working on the experimental and theoretical aspects of crystal growth and its applications in devices. Scope includes: theory of nucleation and growth; molecular kinetics and transport phenomena; crystallization in viscous media (polymers, glasses); crystal growth of metals, minerals, semiconductors, superconductors, magnetic, inorganic, organic, and biological substances in bulk or thin-film form; molecular beam epitaxy (MBE) and chemical vapor deposition (CVD); growth of III-V, II-VI, and other compound semiconductors; characterization of single crystals; apparatus, instrumentation, and purification methods; and multilayer heterostructures with emphasis on epitaxial aspects of electronic materials. A distinguishing feature is its periodic publication of proceedings from major crystal growth conferences (e.g., ICCG, ICMOVPE).

### 2. Crystal Growth & Design (American Chemical Society)
**ISSN:** 1528-7483 | **Publisher:** ACS | **Founded:** 2001

Established by ACS as a lower-cost alternative and complement to the Journal of Crystal Growth, this journal aims to stimulate cross-fertilization of knowledge among scientists and engineers working in crystal growth, crystal engineering, and industrial application of crystalline materials and molecular assemblies. It publishes theoretical and experimental studies of the physical, chemical, and biological phenomena and processes related to the design, growth, and application of crystalline materials, with strong emphasis on synergistic, interdisciplinary approaches (crystal growth, crystal engineering, intermolecular interactions, industrial application). Note: the journal explicitly does **not** accept routine reports of crystal or molecular structures without accompanying scientific insight or a clear novelty narrative.

### 3. Progress in Crystal Growth and Characterization of Materials (Elsevier)
**ISSN:** 0960-8974 | **Publisher:** Elsevier

The only dedicated **review** journal in the crystal growth field. It covers crystal growth and materials characterization, including novel applications, growth methods, and characterization techniques, and acts as a rapid-publication medium for invited review articles and conference reports. Emphasis is placed on practical developments and problems relevant to both academic and industrial researchers. Content is directly commissioned by the Editorial Board (proposal-based submission).

### 4. Crystal Research and Technology (Wiley)
**ISSN:** 0232-1300 (print) / 1521-4079 (online) | **Publisher:** Wiley-VCH

An international journal examining all aspects of research within experimental, industrial, and theoretical crystallography. Coverage includes crystal growth techniques and phenomena (bulk growth and thin films) and modern crystalline materials such as smart materials, nanocrystals, quasicrystals, and liquid crystals. Twelve issues per year.

### 5. Crystals (MDPI)
**ISSN:** 2073-4352 | **Publisher:** MDPI | **Open Access**

An open-access journal covering all aspects of crystalline material research, organized into topical sections. It provides a forum for advancing understanding of the nucleation, growth, processing, and characterization of crystalline and liquid-crystalline materials, along with their mechanical, chemical, electronic, magnetic, and optical properties and applications. Also covers photonic crystals/metamaterials, non-classical growth via particle attachment and fusion, and crystal growth methods relevant to synchrotron and XFEL characterization. Papers reporting only a synthesis and crystal structure without property/application characterization are outside scope.

## Crystallography & Structural Science Journals (IUCr family and related)

### 6. Acta Crystallographica Section A: Foundations and Advances
**ISSN:** 2053-2733 | **Publisher:** International Union of Crystallography (IUCr) / Wiley

Publishes articles on fundamental and mathematical advances in the theory and practice of structural science broadly defined — including nanocrystals, metacrystals, amorphous materials, and quasicrystals in addition to traditional crystallography. Covers electron crystallography, diffuse scattering, pair distribution function studies, time-resolved XFEL studies, cryo-EM, tomography, small-angle scattering, coherent scattering, diffraction imaging, and defect/strain structure. Also welcomes contributions on computational tools, algorithms, software, and AI/machine-learning approaches in structural science.

### 7. Acta Crystallographica Section B: Structural Science, Crystal Engineering and Materials
**ISSN:** 2052-5192 | **Publisher:** IUCr / Wiley

Publishes articles advancing the understanding of structural science as it relates to compounds and materials broadly. It provides a forum for studies exploring how atomic arrangements — and their temporal variation and dependence on temperature, pressure, and other external factors — govern physical and chemical properties and inform the design of new materials and supramolecular systems. Covers both experimental and theoretical approaches in structural science, crystal engineering, and materials research, with emphasis on linking atomic-scale structure to material function.

### 8. Acta Crystallographica Section C: Structural Chemistry
**ISSN:** 2053-2296 | **Publisher:** IUCr / Wiley

A venue for structural chemistry studies where structural insight drives breakthroughs across the chemical sciences. Publishes results obtained through crystallographic techniques (electron, powder, and X-ray diffraction) as well as complementary spectroscopic and computational studies.

### 9. Acta Crystallographica Section D: Structural Biology
**ISSN:** 2059-7983 | **Publisher:** IUCr / Wiley

Covers all aspects of structural biology, with particular emphasis on biological macromolecules and the software/methods used to determine their structures — from the smallest macromolecules to the largest molecular machines. Techniques covered include crystallography, cryo-EM, SAXS, computational structure prediction, NMR, and integrative structural approaches.

### 10. Acta Crystallographica Section E: Crystallographic Communications
**ISSN:** 2056-9890 | **Publisher:** IUCr / Wiley | **Open Access**

A fast, fully open-access structural-communications journal providing a rapid publication mechanism for crystal-structure determinations of inorganic, metal-organic, and organic compounds. The primary category, Research Communications, contains peer-reviewed short reports of one or more structure determinations with appropriate discussion. Also includes an Education and Outreach section covering teaching methods and outreach in structural chemistry.

### 11. Journal of Applied Crystallography (IUCr / Wiley)
**ISSN:** 1600-5767 | **Publisher:** IUCr / Wiley

Scope encompasses applications of crystallography in medicine, biology, chemistry, physics, materials science, and related fields, plus developments in crystallographic instrumentation, apparatus, theory, interpretation, and numerical analysis. Also covers technological applications, computer programs, artificial intelligence methods, and innovations in teaching structural science. Frequently publishes work on crystal-growth methods used to prepare specimens for diffraction/scattering studies (e.g., neutron analyzer crystal growth).

## Crystal Engineering & Chemistry-Adjacent Journals

### 12. CrystEngComm (Royal Society of Chemistry)
**ISSN:** 1466-8033 | **Publisher:** RSC

The forum for the design and understanding of crystalline materials. Welcomes studies on molecular behavior within crystals, control of nucleation and crystal growth, engineering of crystal structures, and construction of crystalline materials with tunable properties and functions. All papers must involve the use or development of a design or optimization strategy — routine structural reports or crystal-morphology descriptions (even combined with basic property/application analysis) are generally outside scope.

### 13. Journal of Chemical Crystallography (Springer)
**ISSN:** 1074-1542 (print) / 1572-8854 (online) | **Publisher:** Springer

An international, interdisciplinary journal dedicated to rapid dissemination of research results in crystallography and spectroscopy. Covers crystal chemistry and physics as they relate to problems of molecular structure; structural studies of solids, liquids, gases, and solutions; and theoretical studies, drawing on spectroscopic, spectrometric, X-ray, electron-, and neutron-diffraction methods.

### 14. Materials (MDPI)
**ISSN:** 1996-1944 | **Publisher:** MDPI | **Open Access**

A broad-scope open-access materials science journal that frequently runs special issues dedicated specifically to crystal growth (e.g., growth and doping of wide-bandgap semiconductors, crystal growth and structure, growth and properties of crystal materials). Relevant for crystal-growth work tied closely to materials engineering and device applications.

## Semiconductor, Applied Physics & Materials Journals Relevant to Crystal Growth

### 15. Journal of Applied Physics (AIP Publishing)
**ISSN:** 0021-8979

A broad-scope physics journal that regularly publishes crystal-growth-related work, particularly on epitaxial growth, semiconductor materials, defect physics, and thin-film properties, when the emphasis is on the underlying physics rather than growth methodology per se.

### 16. Applied Physics Letters (AIP Publishing)
**ISSN:** 0003-6951

A rapid-communication journal for concise reports of significant new findings in applied physics. Frequently publishes short reports on epitaxial and thin-film crystal growth (MBE, MOCVD, etc.) where the growth enables a notable physical or device result.

### 17. Semiconductor Science and Technology (IOP Publishing)
**ISSN:** 0268-1242

Covers the growth, characterization, physics, and device applications of semiconductor materials, including bulk and epitaxial crystal growth of elemental, compound, and low-dimensional semiconductors.

### 18. Journal of Crystal and Molecular Structure (historical; superseded)
Now part of the lineage feeding into modern structural-chemistry journals (historical reference; largely of interest for archival/bibliographic purposes in crystal growth literature reviews).

## Notes on Journal Selection

- **For bulk/industrial crystal growth (melt, solution, vapor growth of semiconductors, oxides, etc.):** *Journal of Crystal Growth*, *Crystal Research and Technology*, *Progress in Crystal Growth and Characterization of Materials*.
- **For crystal engineering, polymorphism, cocrystals, and design-driven crystallization:** *Crystal Growth & Design*, *CrystEngComm*.
- **For structure determination and structural science with crystal-growth components:** the *Acta Crystallographica* family (Sections A–E) and *Journal of Applied Crystallography*.
- **For routine single-crystal structure reports:** *Acta Crystallographica Section E* or *Journal of Chemical Crystallography* are typically the appropriate low-barrier venues (subject to their scope requirements for scientific narrative beyond the structure itself).
- **For epitaxy/thin-film growth tied to device physics:** *Journal of Applied Physics*, *Applied Physics Letters*, *Semiconductor Science and Technology*.
- **For open-access, broad-scope options:** *Crystals* (MDPI) and *Materials* (MDPI), both of which run frequent special issues specifically targeting crystal growth topics.
