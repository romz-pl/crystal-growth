# Georg Müller (Fraunhofer IISB, Erlangen) Bibliography

## Scope and Notes

Georg Müller headed the Crystal Growth Laboratory (CGL) at the Institute of Materials Science, University of Erlangen-Nürnberg, and from 1996 established and led the Crystal Growth working group / department at the Fraunhofer Institute for Integrated Systems and Device Technology (IISB) in Erlangen, Germany, until his retirement in 2007. His work — together with long-time collaborators including G. Neumann, J. Friedrich, D. Vizman, B. Fischer, J. Baumgartl, A. Seidl, M. Kurz, R. Rupp, and others — established much of the foundational understanding of buoyancy-driven, Marangoni, and magnetically-damped convection in melt crystal growth, spanning both physical/laboratory experimentation and numerical/computational modeling (culminating in the CrysVUn/CrysVUn++ and STHAMAS/CrysMAS simulation codes developed at IISB).

This bibliography is organized into two main parts — **Experimental Work** and **Modeling & Simulation Work** — reflecting the dual character of the Erlangen group's research program on melt convection. A third section lists key **Reviews, Books, and Retrospectives** that synthesize or contextualize this body of work. Entries are sorted chronologically (oldest to newest) within each section. Where a single publication contains both substantial experimental and numerical/theoretical content (common in this group's output), it is placed according to its dominant emphasis, with cross-relevance noted.

## Selected Simulation Software Developed at the Crystal Growth Laboratory / Fraunhofer IISB under Müller's Leadership

- **CrysVUn / CrysVUn++** — finite-volume global furnace and crystal growth simulation code, first introduced to the crystal growth community by Fraunhofer IISB in 1999 (M. Kurz, A. Pusztai, G. Müller, *J. Cryst. Growth* 198/199 (1999), pp. 101–106).
- **STHAMAS / STHAMAS3D** — finite-volume code for coupled global heat transport and 3D melt convection simulation, used e.g. for Czochralski and casting process analysis (D. Vizman et al., *J. Cryst. Growth* 303 (2007)).
- **CrysMAS** — successor multi-physics simulation platform for crystal growth, continued and distributed by Fraunhofer IISB after Müller's retirement (documentation: Fraunhofer IISB, download.iisb.fraunhofer.de).
