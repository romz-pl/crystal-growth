# International Conferences for Crystal Growth Research

A curated reference list of the major recurring international conferences, congresses, and workshops where research on crystal growth — spanning bulk crystal growth, epitaxy, industrial crystallization, biological macromolecule crystallization, and crystal growth technology/simulation — can be presented.

---

## 1. International Conference on Crystal Growth and Epitaxy (ICCGE / ICCG)

- **Organizer:** International Organization for Crystal Growth (IOCG)
- **Frequency:** Every 3 years
- **History:** The founding conference of the field, started as ICCG-1 in Boston, USA, in 1966. It marked the establishment of crystal growth as an independent scientific discipline rather than a subsidiary topic within physics, chemistry, or materials science. Now in its 20s (ICCGE-21 was held in Xi'an, China, in August 2025; ICCGE-22 is planned in conjunction with the 20th International Summer School on Crystal Growth).
- **Scope and aims:** The flagship, most comprehensive conference in the field. It brings together the global crystal growth community to present and discuss recent research and development across essentially all aspects of crystal growth, epitaxy, characterization, and applications. Core topics include crystal growth theory and modeling, bulk semiconductor materials, thin-film epitaxy, laser and nonlinear optical crystals, biocrystals, nanoscale growth (nanocrystals, nanowires, nanomaterials), and more. The program is organized into general sessions plus numerous topical symposia (typically ~19 parallel sections), covering the full range of materials, growth methods, and applications. It is explicitly built on the principle that the theory and practice of crystal growth is shared across many materials systems, so practitioners from different subfields have much to learn from one another.
- **Relevance:** The premier general-scope venue for any crystal growth article, whether theoretical, computational, or experimental.

## 2. American Conference on Crystal Growth and Epitaxy (ACCGE / AACGE)

- **Organizer:** American Association for Crystal Growth (AACG), under the auspices of IOCG
- **Frequency:** Biennial, alternating between the broader ACCGE and the more narrowly focused ACCGE-West
- **History:** Long-running U.S. national conference series (ACCGE-24 was held in Stevenson, WA in July 2025; ACCGE-23 in Tucson, AZ in 2023), frequently held jointly with the U.S. Workshop on Organometallic Vapor Phase Epitaxy (OMVPE).
- **Scope and aims:** Serves as the confluence of "all things crystal growth and epitaxy" for the American crystal growth community, though international participation is welcomed. Covers growth methods (melt, vapor, solution, etc.), material types (semiconductors, biological materials, oxides, etc.), dimensionality (bulk, thin films, 2D, nanostructures), and functional properties (optical, electronic, optoelectronic, ferroelectric). Provides a forum for scientists, engineers, and researchers from academia, industry, and government laboratories to discuss the latest advances, integrating fundamentals with experimental/industrial growth processes, characterization, and applications.
- **Note:** The companion **AACG-West** conference has a narrower regional/topical focus and addresses current and emerging challenges in understanding, engineering, and design of crystal growth in nature and technology, with contributed sessions on nucleation, crystallization thermodynamics/kinetics, self- and directed-assembly, morphology and interfacial evolution, and computational modeling of crystal growth.

## 3. European Conference on Crystal Growth (ECCG)

- **Organizer:** European Network of Crystal Growth (ENCG), in association with IOCG and national crystal-growth societies
- **Frequency:** Every 3 years
- **History:** Series held in cities including Paris, Varna, Bologna, Glasgow, Budapest, and Warsaw (ECCG-8, July 2024); ECCG-9 is scheduled for Berlin in 2027.
- **Scope and aims:** The main pan-European venue for crystal growth science, inviting all scientists involved in crystal growth and crystal characterization. Topics span the theory and practice of crystal growth, with recent editions highlighting strong growth in the modeling/simulation of physical phenomena governing the formation of perfect and imperfect crystalline materials, and the emerging application of artificial intelligence to crystal growth research. The ENCG's broader mission is to strengthen collaboration between academia and industry in Europe and to foster education in crystal growth theory and technology.
- **Companion event:** The **European School on Crystal Growth (ESCG)** runs alongside ECCG (since 2015) as an educational program of extended lectures on fundamentals and recent discoveries in crystal growth, aimed particularly at students and early-career researchers.

## 4. International Workshop on Crystal Growth Technology (IWCGT)

- **Organizer:** Leibniz-Institut für Kristallzüchtung (IKZ), Berlin, under the auspices of IOCG and the German Association for Crystal Growth (DGKK)
- **Frequency:** Roughly every 2–4 years
- **History:** Initiated in 1998 by Hans Scheel and Tsuguo Fukuda; IWCGT-9 was held in Berlin-Adlershof in June 2026.
- **Scope and aims:** Distinct from the more academic conferences above, this workshop is devoted specifically to *crystal growth technology* — the application-oriented, industrial-scale side of the field, including production technologies, machining, and equipment, with a focus on the preparation of bulk and substrate crystals (e.g., growth technology for electronics/photovoltaics materials such as Si, Ge, and III–V compounds; wide-bandgap semiconductor substrates; piezoelectric, laser, and luminescent crystals). Its explicit aim is to link R&D to actual production, bridging science and industrial practice. The format favors in-depth invited lectures from renowned experts plus poster sessions and extensive discussion/networking time, rather than a high-volume contributed-talk program.
- **Relevance:** Well suited for articles with an applied/industrial or engineering-technology angle on crystal growth, including scale-up and process modeling.

## 5. International Symposium on Industrial Crystallization (ISIC) and BIWIC Workshop

### ISIC — International Symposium on Industrial Crystallization
- **Organizer:** European Federation of Chemical Engineering (EFCE), Working Party on Crystallization
- **Frequency:** Triennial
- **Scope and aims:** The leading international symposium on industrial crystallization processes, organized around themes such as fundamentals of crystallization, crystallization/precipitation in fine chemical, specialty, and life-science industries, developments in large-scale industrial crystallization, contributions of crystallization to sustainability, and integrated process design (crystallization within the industrial process chain). It also serves as a venue for student presentations and industry–exhibitor engagement (e.g., ISIC 2026 in Prague, Czech Republic).

### BIWIC — International Workshop on Industrial Crystallization
- **Organizer:** Rotating academic/industrial hosts, coordinated within the crystallization community (originally "Bremen International Workshop on Industrial Crystallization")
- **Frequency:** Annual
- **History:** First held in Bremen, Germany, in 1990; subsequent editions have been hosted in Germany, France, China, South Korea, Denmark, South Africa, the Netherlands, Thailand, Finland, Sweden, and Switzerland (BIWIC 2026 in Winterthur).
- **Scope and aims:** Brings together researchers and engineers from academia and industry to discuss recent discoveries and challenges in industrial crystallization. Typical topics include fundamentals of crystallization (solid–liquid equilibrium, nucleation, crystal growth kinetics), polymorphism and co-crystals, nanoparticle formation, product/particle design and characterization, freeze/antisolvent/reactive/melt crystallization, and the design, scale-up, and control of industrial crystallizers, with recent editions emphasizing circular-economy and resource-recovery applications.
- **Relevance:** These two venues are the primary international outlets for crystal growth work framed around industrial/process crystallization (solution and melt crystallization at scale) rather than semiconductor or optical single-crystal growth.

## 6. International Conference on the Crystallization of Biological Macromolecules (ICCBM)

- **Organizer:** International Organization for Biological Crystallization (IOBCr), founded 2002
- **Frequency:** Biennial
- **History:** First held in 1985 at Stanford, California; ICCBM17 (Shanghai, 2018) and ICCBM18 (Tempe, Arizona, 2024) are recent editions.
- **Scope and aims:** The principal international meeting dedicated to the crystallization of proteins, nucleic acids, and other biological macromolecules for structural biology. It aims to exchange research results and promote practical applications of biological crystallization and structural determination, attracting crystallographers, biochemists, physicists, and engineers. Topics include nucleation studies, new detection techniques for crystalline precipitates, production of nano- and micro-crystal suspensions in support of X-ray free-electron laser (XFEL) data collection, membrane protein crystallization, and complementary structural methods such as cryo-EM.
- **Relevance:** The appropriate venue if the crystal growth work concerns biomolecular/protein crystallization rather than inorganic or semiconductor crystals.

## 7. Related and Adjacent Venues

These are not exclusively crystal-growth conferences but regularly include substantial crystal growth content and are worth considering depending on the specific material system or angle of the work:

- **IUCr Congress and General Assembly** (International Union of Crystallography) — the largest global crystallography congress, held every 3 years, with dedicated commissions and sessions on "Crystal Growth and Characterization of Materials."
- **German Association for Crystal Growth (DGKK) Annual Conference** — national conference with strong crystal growth content, often co-organizing workshops such as IWCGT and WODIL (German–French Workshop on Oxide, Dielectric, and Laser Crystals).
- **British Association for Crystal Growth (BACG) Annual Conference** — UK-based, covering the theory, practice, and characterization of crystal growth and crystallization.
- **Materials Research Society (MRS) Spring/Fall Meetings** — very large multidisciplinary materials conferences that regularly feature dedicated symposia on crystal growth, epitaxy, and semiconductor materials.
- **Journal of Crystal Growth-affiliated conferences** — several of the conferences above (ICCGE, ECCG, ICCBM) publish dedicated proceedings/special issues in the *Journal of Crystal Growth* or *Crystal Growth & Design*, which is a useful signal of a conference's centrality to the field.
